---

The Delay step is an action step that pauses workflow execution for a specified duration before continuing to the next step. It gives you precise control over notification timing — allowing you to schedule follow-up messages, wait for user responses, or defer delivery until a specific moment.

When a workflow reaches a Delay step, execution pauses. Once the delay condition is satisfied, the workflow resumes and all downstream steps execute normally.

## How Delay Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Workflow reaches the Delay step">
    Execution pauses at the Delay node. The workflow is placed in a waiting state for the configured duration.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Duration elapses">
    Novu monitors the delay timer. Once the configured duration passes (or the scheduled time arrives), the workflow is resumed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Downstream steps execute">
    All steps after the Delay step run normally, with access to the original trigger payload.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Steps placed **before** the Delay step run immediately when the workflow is triggered. Steps placed **after** the Delay step run only after the delay completes.
</Info>

## When to Use Delay

The Delay step is the right choice any time you want to introduce a pause between notification steps:

| Use Case | Delay Duration |
|---|---|
| Wait before sending a push, in case the user already saw the in-app notification | 5–15 minutes |
| Send a follow-up email if a user hasn't confirmed their email address | 1–2 hours |
| Remind a user about an abandoned shopping cart | 24 hours |
| Send an onboarding check-in after registration | 3 days |
| Deliver a renewal reminder before a subscription expires | 7–14 days |

## Add a Delay Step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Workflow Editor">
    Go to the [Novu Dashboard](https://dashboard.novu.co), navigate to **Workflows**, and open or create a workflow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the Delay step">
    Click the **+** icon at the position in the workflow where you want to introduce a pause. Select **Delay** from the action steps panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the delay duration">
    Set the delay amount and unit — for example, 30 minutes, 2 hours, 1 day, or a specific scheduled time. Delay can also read duration values from the trigger payload for dynamic scheduling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add steps after the delay">
    Add channel steps (email, in-app, SMS, push, or chat) after the Delay node. These steps execute once the delay period has elapsed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example: In-App Then Follow-Up Email

A common pattern is to send an immediate in-app notification, then follow up by email if needed:

```
Trigger
  └── In-App (immediate — shown right away)
  └── Delay (24 hours)
  └── Email (sent 24 hours after trigger)
```

This gives users a chance to see the in-app message first. If they act on it, you can use step conditions on the email step to skip the follow-up entirely.

## Combining Delay with Step Conditions

You can attach step conditions to any step that comes after a Delay. For example:

- Skip the email step if the in-app notification was already read
- Only send the SMS if the subscriber has not logged in during the delay period
- Escalate to push only if the email was not opened

This makes Delay a powerful building block for adaptive, multi-step notification flows.

## Delay vs. Digest

Both Delay and Digest control timing, but they serve different purposes:

| Feature | Delay | Digest |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Pause execution for a fixed time | Batch multiple events into one |
| Triggers affected | Every trigger waits independently | Multiple triggers are merged |
| Output | One notification per trigger, sent later | One notification summarizing many triggers |
| Use case | Follow-up messages, reminders | Activity summaries, burst protection |

<Note>
  For full configuration options — including payload-driven dynamic delays and scheduled delivery — refer to the [Configure Delay Step](https://docs.novu.co/platform/workflow/add-and-configure-steps/configure-action-steps/delay) documentation.
</Note>
